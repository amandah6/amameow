Banana bread

Ingredients

  • 250g all purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 150g brown sugar

  • 2 large eggs (room temperature)

  • 80g sour cream or greek yogurt
  • 345g bananas (usually 3 bananas)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

  • 90g mix-ins (chopped nuts and/or dark chocolate chips)

Directions

  1. Combine flour, baking soda, salt, and ground cinnamon in a bowl
  2. Cream together butter and brown sugar in a separte bowl
  3. Beat in 2 large eggs, one at a time
  4. Mash bananas in a third bowl, mix in sour cream and vanilla
  5. Thoroughly combine banana mixture with butter, sugar, egg mixuture
  6. Fold in flour mixture until just combined
  7. Add mix-ins until just combined, do not overmix
  8. Spray loaf pan with non-stick cooking spray and dump batter in
  9. Bake at 350ºF for 60-65 minutes on oven rack in the lower 3rd position

Notes

  • I make this almost every Sunday
  • I use light and dark brown sugar interchangeably in this recipe, can’t really feel the difference
  • Life hack: soften butter in the microwave, warm up eggs by putting in warm/hot water

Chef John's Lemon Bars

12/30/25

Source

Ingredients

Shortbread crust

  • 120g all purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (softened)

  • 28.25g confectioner’s sugar
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Lemon curd

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 egg yolk

  • 2 tbsp all purpose flour
  • 198g granulated sugar

  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp lemon zest

Directions

Shortbread crust

  1. Mash butter and flour together in a bowl until thoroughly combined
  2. Mix in confectioner’s sugar, vanilla, and salt until fully combined
  3. Grease an 8x8 cake pan and press crust mixture into the pan, prick bottom with a fork
  4. Bake at 350ºF for 22 minutes

Wait for crust to finish baking before starting next step 1

Lemon curd
5. In a large bowl, beat together 2 large eggs and 1 egg yolk 6. Whisk in granulated sugar and 2 tbsp of all-purpose flour until smooth 2 7. Whisk in lemon juice an lemon zest for 2 minutes 8. Pour over cooled crust, bake at 350ºF for 25 minutes

Notes

  • This recipe produces about a 1:1 ratio of crust to lemon, which I find perfect

  1. If you prepare the lemon curd while the crust is baking and dump it right into the hot crust, you will cook the curd touching the crust too fast and it’ll be eggy ↩︎

  2. I like lemon bars more lemony and less sweet, I’ve been able to reduce the granulated sugar by about -40g and replace it with a tablespoon or so of flour and a splash more lemon juice without affecting the texture noticeabley ↩︎

Cornbread

Ingredients

  • 150g all-purpose flour
  • 117g yellow cornmeal
  • 49.5g granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt

  • 1 1/4 cups whole milk
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large egg (beaten)

Directions

  1. Combine flour, cornmeal, sugar, baking powder, and salt in a large bowl
  2. In a measuring cup, measure out milk, vegetable oil, and beat in 1 egg
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry, mixing until combined, don’t overmix
  4. Spray an 8x8 or 9x9 inch cake pan with non-stick cooking spray
  5. Pour in batter and bake for 25-30 min at 400ºF

Notes

  • Goes hard with chili or baked beans + weens
  • I add more sugar for sweet cornbread, add shredded cheese, green onion, and minced garlic for a savory bread
  • Moist and buttery without soaking cornmeal! I overbaked it once and it was very dry though
  • I rarely buy milk, so a lot of the time I mix 1/2 cup half n half with 3/4 cups of water to make “whole milk” and it turns out okay

Lemon Shortbread Cookies

3/8/26

Source

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup butter (softened)
  • 65g granulated sugar
  • zest of 1 medium lemon

  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

  • 180g all purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Directions

  1. Cream together butter, granulated sugar and lemon zest
  2. Mix in lemon juice and vanilla extract until fully combined
  3. Add flour and salt and mix until just combined
  4. Flatten dough into a disk, wrap and chill in the freezer for 15-30 minutes or until slightly firm
  5. Roll dough between two sheets of parchment paper until 1/4 in. thick, cut into 2 in. squares or use a 2 in. diameter cookie cutter
  6. Place on baking sheet 2 in. apart and bake at 325ºF for 14-16 minutes or until lightly browned on the edges

Notes

  • I love these cookies!
  • Bake them slightly longer for melt-in-your-mouth texture

Oatmeal Cookies

Ingredients

  • 125g all purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 100g granulated sugar
  • 110g light brown sugar

  • 1 large egg

  • 1tsp vanilla extract
  • 120g oatmeal
  • 180g mix-ins (chocolate chips, nuts, etc)

Directions

  1. Combine flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt, and ground cinnamon in a bowl
  2. Cream together butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar in a separte bowl
  3. Beat in 1 large egg and vanilla extract
  4. Mix in flour mixture until just combined
  5. Add oatmeal and mix-ins until just combined, do not overmix
  6. Roll into 2 tbsp balls and place on baking sheet 2 inches apart and bake at 375ºF for 8-10 minutes

Notes

  • Yummy with chocolate chips but also very yummy without
  • Have only ever made once, but tasty! Pretty sweet, would make with less sugar next time

Peanut Butter Cookies

Ingredients

  • 128g peanut butter
  • 8 tablespoons butter (softened)
  • 146.5g brown sugar
  • 50g granulated sugar
  • 6.5g vanilla extract

  • 1 large egg (room temp)

  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 152g all purpose flour

Directions

  • Cream together peanut butter, butter, brown sugar, granulated sugar, and vanilla extract
  • Beat in 1 large egg
  • Mix in flour, baking powder, and salt until combined
  • Roll into 2 tbsp balls and place on baking sheet 2 inches apart and bake at 350ºF for 12-13 minutes

Notes

  • Usually makes me 13-17 cookies
  • Tap the baking sheet onto the edge of the oven a couple times if the cookies look really puffed up, lets them deflate and develop the cracked look?
  • Have made multiple times! Soft, chewy, grocery store cookie vibes!
